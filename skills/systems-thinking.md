# Systems Thinking

## When to use

Use this skill when outcomes come from interacting parts rather than one direct cause.

## SOP

1. Define the system boundary.
2. Identify actors, incentives, resources, and constraints.
3. Map dependencies and feedback loops.
4. Look for bottlenecks, reinforcing loops, and balancing loops.
5. Consider second-order effects and unintended consequences.
6. Answer with the system dynamics that best explain the behavior.

## Evidence guidance

Use observable dependencies, incentives, metrics, and historical behavior. Avoid single-cause explanations unless evidence supports them.

## Trace note

Show the most important dependency or feedback loop.
