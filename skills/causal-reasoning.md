# Causal Reasoning

## When to use

Use this skill when the user asks why something happened or whether one factor caused another.

## SOP

1. State the proposed cause and effect.
2. Separate correlation from mechanism.
3. Check timing, plausibility, and confounders.
4. Consider alternative causes.
5. Ask what intervention would change the outcome.
6. State whether causality is established, plausible, or speculative.

## Evidence guidance

Prefer controlled comparisons, direct measurements, natural experiments, and mechanistic evidence over anecdotes.

## Trace note

Show the causal status: established, plausible, or speculative.
