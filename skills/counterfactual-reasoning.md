# Counterfactual Reasoning

## When to use

Use this skill when a conclusion depends on an assumption that may be false.

## SOP

1. Identify the premise being tested.
2. Describe the current-world explanation.
3. Describe the alternative world where the premise changes.
4. Compare what predictions differ between the two worlds.
5. Look for evidence that distinguishes them.
6. State which premise is more consistent with the evidence.

## Evidence guidance

Prefer evidence that would be unlikely under one scenario and likely under another.

## Trace note

Show the counterfactual premise being tested.
